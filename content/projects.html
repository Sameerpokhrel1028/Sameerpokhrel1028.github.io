Research

Ongoing PhD Research

Current research activities integrating plant breeding, genomics, and pangenome-based analysis for peanut improvement.

Project Lead

Pangenome construction and variants observation
Interactive visualization of genome-wide and regional variant density across peanut genomes.
Pan-MAGIC Platform for Peanut Improvement
Development of an 18-founder MAGIC population (8-way and 16-way), pangenome construction, variant discovery, and population-scale genomic analysis.
Development of Drought-Tolerant Peanut Lines
Breeding and evaluation of drought-tolerant peanut germplasm; 2–3 highly tolerant lines ready for release.
Development of White Mold–Resistant Peanut Lines
Integration of field phenotyping and genomic analysis for resistance to white mold; expected germplasm release in Spring 2027.

As a Collaborator

TSWV Resistance Loci Identification
Mapping and characterization of loci associated with Tomato Spotted Wilt Virus resistance in peanut.
Wild Introgression & Nematode Resistance Gene Discovery
Identification of introgressed genomic regions and candidate genes conferring nematode resistance.
Novel Tar-Spot Disease Fungus Identification
Characterization and identification of a novel tar-spot disease fungus affecting peanut.
Long-Read Low-Pass Sequencing Technology Development
Development and evaluation of long-read, low-coverage sequencing strategies for cost-effective breeding applications.
