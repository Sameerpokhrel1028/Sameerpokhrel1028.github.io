About

I am a PhD researcher in Plant Breeding & Genomics at the University of Georgia, working on genetic improvement of peanut (Arachis hypogaea) with a focus on drought tolerance, white mold resistance, and utilization of wild introgression for crop improvement.

A major component of my work involves developing drought-tolerant and white moldâ€“resistant peanut lines through breeding, integrating field phenotyping with genomic analyses to identify and advance superior genotypes. I work across multiple generations and populations, linking phenotypic performance with underlying genomic regions.

My research integrates pangenome and graph-based genomics with population-scale variant analysis to detect introgressed segments from wild relatives and characterize genomic regions associated with stress tolerance and disease resistance. I work extensively with long-read and short-read sequencing data, structural variation, and regional variant density profiling.

Overall, my interests lie at the intersection of applied plant breeding, comparative genomics, and data visualization, with the goal of translating genomic insights into practical tools and strategies for peanut improvement.
